Denise

Denise cut her teeth in advertising working alongside creative giants at Cutwater in San Francisco. Her time there shaped her approach to advertising: Make work you would proudly put on your wall in your home. With that goal in mind, she strives to turn ads into work that is beloved by the target it is designed to reach, and in doing so, connecting on a much more meaningful level. In addition to her day job, Denise teaches at Miami Ad School to help young creatives learn the ropes of  the industry. She is also a board member of the ADCC.

Julie

My first bank account was with TD because that’s where my parents banked so I didn’t give it a second thought. When I got older and started thinking seriously about my portfolio, I started looking critically at my bank and realized I was paying them for the work I was doing. Currently I still use TD for their web broker features but I mostly use Tangerine for my credit, RRSPS, and other savings products because paying to save makes zero sense.

Kira

Kira is a consumer experience fanatic, with a passion for leading brands to what’s next at the intersect of people, platforms, and culture. She has a unique mix of experience leading strategy teams both creative and media side, where she’s contributed to award winning work for Chevrolet, Coca-Cola, and Aurora Cannabis. At age 30, Kira was named VP of Strategy at one of the country’s largest media agencies – making her the youngest strategy head in a global network of over 45,000. But a burning desire to be back at the creative table, influencing ideas at inception and across the full consumer journey, led her back to her roots.

Our primary mission when it comes to media planning and buying, is to deliver our partners better business results from their media investments. We achieve this by:
  1. Removing the inefficiencies that result from creating islands of the people overseeing various aspects of the process and instead placing them shoulder to shoulder in order to ideate, create and iterate for better results at speed.
  2. Advocating for better media conversations & KPIs by bringing conversions back to measurable business and marketing outcomes across the full funnel and consumer journey.
  3. Giving our partners greater transparency into media decisions and fees; there are no black boxes here – planning fees are separated from buying fees so that you get clear line of sight to the exact return on every dollar invested in media.
  4. Chasing better business outcomes and results, not vanity metrics and lower (race to the bottom) media rates
